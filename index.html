Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Progressive enhancement is an approach to developing on the Internet that prioritizes content instead of design. After content is created in HTML, CSS and then JavaScript can be added to enhance visuals and experience. There are many reasons why this is important in the modern web. The first reason is accessibility. In the old system of graceful degredation, accessibility standards were not met. Old browsers were pushed to the back burner all the while design and content were intertwined, not allowing people who needed to remove the design to be able to do so. Progressive enhancment is the kind of development we have learned in class-- HTML content before CSS. Here is a link to another article about progressive enhancement: Freecodecamp.org
