Most plants might be vegetables

What is the difference between a fruit and vegetable?

According to healthline.com, A fruit develops from the flower of a plant, while the other parts of the plant are categorized as vegetables.

The importance

This defintions has major implications: every single plant is a vegetable if it is not a fruit. So is a tree branch a vegetable? A pine needle? A whole tree? We will discuss and find out.

What specificially is a vegetable?

Healthline.com gave these possible three characteristics of a vegetable:

  1. Roots
  2. Stems
  3. Leaves

That website has lots of contentious information, but it attempts to solve it by providing more specific definitions. Here is a quote from the author:

Botanically, fruits contain seeds and come from the flower of a plant, while the rest of the plant is considered a vegetable. In cooking, fruits are considered to be sweet while vegetables are more savory.
From healthline.com written by Rachael Link, MS, RD

So in sum--

Vegetable
Literally any part of plant that isnt a fruit.
Fruit
Things that come from flowers.
Botanical Vegetable
Traditonal food from a plant that is savory.
Botanical Fruit
Traditional food from a flower that are sweet.

Characteristics of each kind
Food Vegetables Fruits Botanical vegetables Botanical fruits

Examples of fruits vs veggies

Vegetables:

  • Carrots
  • Lettuce
  • Bay leaf
  • Tree bark
  • Trees
  • Bushes

Fruits:

  • Apples
  • Bananas
  • Pears
The take away

Vegetables are all plants that are not fruits. Does that mean you should eat them? Maybe not-- you decide.
